The one-liner that sells

BioSense is the system that knows your biology, your goals, and your limits. It explains your panels with patience and precision, tracks what changed between draws, and only recommends moves that fit you: allergies, training load, family risk, the objectives you said matter.

What we actually build

1. Lab brain (the engine)

  • Structured ingest for your chosen biomarker panels: PDF or structured feeds, with a written spec so labs and software never drift apart.
  • Dashboard analysis people read: prioritised, contextual, tied to reference and optimal bands, not a wall of abbreviations.
  • Longitudinal intelligence: second and later draws versus baseline. What moved, why it matters, what to watch before the next test.
  • Hard guardrails: recommendations filtered through Your profile & objectives so the product never suggests what you already ruled out.

2. Profile & objectives (non-negotiable)

  • Visual sections: allergies, exercise, family history, goals, lifestyle, all editable any time.
  • Not decoration: those fields steer the model (athlete vs general ranges, stress on liver markers, and more).

3. AI agent (depth + speed)

  • Conversational onboarding and adaptive questionnaires so day one is not a forty-field form.
  • Roadmap logic: the agent knows what it needs to propose a credible improvement path and asks for it in plain language.
  • Smart reports (auto from labs + profile, e.g. fastest wins) and custom reports on demand (“top five biomarkers I can move first, give me the plan”).
  • Navigation: “Show my reports” resolves to the right screen. Small for UX, huge for retention.
  • Business answers: subscriptions, regions, how to book. Table stakes for a consumer brand.

4. Dashboard (the home screen)

  • Upcoming home draw, biological age, biomarkers, actionable reports, “How am I doing?” journey, upgrade path: the full spine from your brief.
  • “How am I doing?” as motivation: trajectory, milestones, framing you agree with your clinical lead so numbers stay defensible.

5. Trust, ops, scale

  • Sample sanity signals when a panel looks internally inconsistent or duplicated, routed to BioSense ops. Support for the lab, not a replacement.
  • Data residency for UAE and neighbours: named cloud regions and vendor options on paper, not vibes.
  • Email PDF of actionable summaries: shareable, high trust.
  • Proactive nudges: monthly rhythm, tone and cadence owned by you, powered by the same intelligence layer.

Straight talk (builds credibility)

  • A marketed “success rate vs doctors” is a clinical and legal claim, not a GitHub ticket. Engineering follows the validation path you and counsel set.
  • Population lines like “X% less likely to develop Y” need real data or licensed baselines. We can ship the experience; the numbers need a source of truth.
  • Function Health tier polish is absolutely in play as a parallel track: UX, content, and design system, not only models.
